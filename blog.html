30/01/2020 Update
This site will contain some insight into projects
FSJ Abschlussseminar (July 2018)
During this group trip to Lake Garda in Italy I decided to make a travel blog, initially planned with narration. Again filmed on a phone and sometimes over-graded in the edit. I wanted to capture my experience of it, hoped to capture it for the others as well, and I love looking back at it. I discovered that I really had a love for landscape videography.
Syeh's Exile (May 2018)
Syeh's Exile was more or less my first finished narrative video. At this naive early stage I had a lot of fun coming up with all the lore and storyboards. I also got to play around a lot with editing and colour grading. The film had a lot of musical influences that I shared with my soundtrack friend. Syeh's Exile was when I started reading up on film a lot, but it comes from this innocent time of just barely knowing some basics. Looking back I can still enjoy what I was trying to do.
Holding Still (April 2018)
This love letter to Iceland was again a strange script journey as I learned frameworks that I applied during the writing. This was the first script that I spent a good amount of time outlining. Notte is also the first character I wrote that I really felt and admired. The dialogue is terrible and some things in it are really cringy, yet I still feel the story has something sweet and naive about it, since that's really how I would describe Notte. The script is very alive in my head and I wish I could have it filmed, just to see how it works. I want it to be as beautiful as I picture it.
Infiltration (December 2017)
Unfortunately the script idea that got me into screenwriting collapsed under its scope too quickly, therefore my first completed screenplay was Infiltration. During the writing of Infiltration I started learning about a few screenwriting frameworks, which gradually were applied to the story. At the time I had a hard time finding out what worked and what didn't work and rewriting was short and difficult.
Lahr - A Hometown (December 2017)
I never appreciated my hometown until a few months before leaving it. In an attempt to capture what it means to me I decided to do a brief story of my life video about it. I think a lot about geography so I decided to do it via a map and images through 'green screened' cards coming in and out. Due to it all being filmed one handed on a phone the quality is questionable and keyframing took me two weeks, yet I still look at this project with a smile.
