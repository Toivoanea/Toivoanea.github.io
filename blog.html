07/04/2019 Sangseunghan - First Draft
Holding Still had its anniversary on the 6th of April. The day after its release I started working on Sangseunghan, thereby marking the 7th as the anniversary of its inception. Now, exactly a year later, I can present the First Draft. This first draft is already a reworked version of the original outline, though from now on I want to collect feedback from others and conclude what worked, what I can improve and what things I will have to consider for future screenplays.
Sangseunghan developed out of a simple showerthought: A story about a person who has dreams and nightmares that parallel each other greatly. Fairly quickly this concept grew with the themes of utopia and dystopia.
I knew that for this project I wanted to "dream", meaning that I wanted to write it as if I had all the money in the world to make it. Obviously this is a screenplay that due to high visual effects and niche story would be commercially impossible to make, but this was never the goal.
Phoebe as a character came after the concept, which is probably apparent, as to me it sometimes feels like she is a bit too passive to be a good protagonist, as the story is not as character driven. Nonetheless I find her story fascinating and am still proud of it. Initially I wanted to force myself to improve my dialogue writing by having a dialogue heavy story. While I like writing conflict scenes, I have to work on writing stories that require story and character arcs to play out in non-verbal ways.
Sangseunghan is a love letter to Neo-Futurist architecture and those wonderful TED Talks about future city planning, which have greatly inspired more thought and created this setting.
Why Korea? I wanted to go somewhere else. Neo Tokyo has too many iterations and the future western world was not really something I have high hopes for. South Korea is placed that due to its separated culture is mainly overlooked by many. I wanted to imagine a world where the world's governments went away from the thought of countries, and where the Koreas come together with the rest of the world to form an accepting, multicultural place. Sangseunghan is still a very optimistic future, but I wanted to present a positive image of how the future could be, to inspire, rather than scare.
24/02/2019 thoughts on achieving my dream
where do you go when you achived your dreams?

it sounds like a petty worry of someone happy.

but does it actually make you happy?

for a while it's empowering and euphoric

until you lose that euphoria and you keep telling yourself:

"you're living the dream, be happy, you've made it"

you soon come to realise you're lying to yourself

i'm flawed; the past taught me wrong.

when life was not worth living i wanted to look back

but there was nothing to look back at in awe.

so i learned to live for the future, for hope; toivoa.

and some day you come to realise,

that this hope for a better future is foolish.

the happiness will never last, because we

get so used to our circumstances.

but how is it possible to live the moment?

when you've lived your whole life for the future?

can i change, or is this rooted inside me?

should i change to live the day?

should i keep living the lie?

i am lost

the world at my feet,

but without a clue where to go.

30/03/2018 Holding Still Update #11
What was previously known as Ljósálfar now has a proper title: "Holding Still".
Major addition: ACT II has been completed, finally. Now the screenplay is gapless.
'Return' has been compacted and refined. 'Elven cave' as well, I've also added memory loss as a way of having consequences in her action. I personally think it feels fitting in a story about moments, to have moments lost. It's not too sad either as they can make new ones. Basically everything got its tweaks.
Smilla has been renamed to Felinne, for personal reasons and because of the way it sounds and what effect it might have, also because "Notte and Smilla" sounded weird as a couple to me, so I wanted to change that.
Felinne and Notte's ages have been changed to 19 to make Felinne's story believable, therefore they are now students.
